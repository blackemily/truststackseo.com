How to read an ingredient list

First, an ingredient list is not a promise list. It does not guarantee outcomes. It only shows what is included.

Second, supplements are usually built as groups. One group may support general balance. Another group may support cellular support. Another group may support digestion and uptake.

Third, dosage and quality matter. Two products can list the same ingredient. They can still be very different.

How to judge Mitolyn ingredients fairly

Use a simple test plan. Keep it boring. Boring tests are honest tests.

Step 1: write down your baseline

For three days, rate your energy on a 1 to 10 scale. Do it four times a day. Morning. Late morning. Afternoon. Evening.

Step 2: keep big changes out

If you start a new diet and a new workout and a new supplement, you learn nothing. Keep routine stable during the test.

Step 3: track for at least two weeks

Two weeks is a minimum for patterns. Four weeks is better. Steady support takes time.

Step 4: look for stability

Do not look for a “high” feeling. Look for fewer dips. Look for fewer crashes. Look for smoother days.
